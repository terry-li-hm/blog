A cell is the most complex system in the known universe. It runs thousands of simultaneous reactions, builds proteins from blueprints, repairs its own DNA, maintains structural integrity under stress, and reproduces.

It does all of this without a general-purpose reasoning engine. No prediction. No pattern matching. No generation. Every reaction works through shape -- a molecule fits a substrate, the reaction fires. Thermodynamics and probability do the rest.

There is no LLM in a cell.

I've been building a system I call vivesca -- a persistent layer that sits between me and the AI models I use. I had been calling the LLMs "enzymes." Enzymes are native to the cell. Evolved, specialized, permanent. The cell needs its enzymes.

That framing is wrong.

An LLM is external, general-purpose, and temporary. It's not an enzyme. It's a prosthetic.

You use a crutch while the bone heals. You use an LLM while the deterministic pathway crystallizes. The goal is to stop needing it for that reaction.

The lifecycle looks like this:

Experience -> Memory -> Knowledge -> Program -> Unnecessary.

"Unnecessary" is the prosthetic coming off. The bone healed. The hook fires on shape alone. The tool runs without judgment. The reaction that once required a billion-parameter model now runs as a five-line script triggered by pattern match.

What the organism actually wants from the prosthetic: handle things it has never seen before. Catalyze the first reaction so the cell can observe the pattern. Then get out of the way while the cell crystallizes it into real machinery.

What the organism should not want: permanent dependency. The prosthetic becoming load-bearing. Designing around the prosthetic's strengths instead of the cell's needs.

The design pressure isn't "use the LLM better." It's "need the LLM less."

Every hook that replaces a prompt, every tool that replaces a judgment call, every program that replaces a conversation -- that's a bone healing. That's the cell building its own machinery.

The endgame isn't a better prosthetic. It's a cell so well-built that the prosthetic only handles genuinely novel substrates -- things the organism has never metabolized before. Everything else is instinct.
